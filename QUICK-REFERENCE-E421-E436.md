## Source Breakdown

### Natural/Synthetic (Can be either)
- **E421 Mannitol**: Naturally in mushrooms/seaweed, commercially from fructose hydrogenation
- **E422 Glycerol**: Naturally in all fats/oils, commercially from vegetable oils/biodiesel or petrochemical

### Plant-Derived (Natural)
- **E425 Konjac**: From konjac root (Amorphophallus konjac)
- **E426 Soybean hemicellulose**: From soybean fiber/okara (tofu waste)
- **E427 Cassia gum**: From cassia seeds

### Animal-Derived
- **E428 Gelatin**: From animal collagen (pork skin 60%, beef bones/hide 30%, fish 10%)

### Synthetic
- **E431**: Stearic acid + polyethylene glycol
- **E432-436**: Polysorbates (sorbitol + fatty acid + ethylene oxide)

## Special Warnings & Restrictions

### E425 Konjac
⚠️ **CHOKING HAZARD**: Mini-cup jelly containing konjac BANNED in EU (2004) and USA  
📅 Multiple choking deaths led to bans  
👶 Especially dangerous for children and elderly

### E428 Gelatin
🐷 **Pork gelatin** (60% of market): NOT halal, NOT kosher, NOT vegetarian  
🐄 **Beef gelatin** (30%): Halal/kosher ONLY if from certified slaughter  
🐟 **Fish gelatin** (10%): Kosher (pareve), pescatarian-friendly  
🌱 **Vegan alternatives**: Agar (E406), Pectin (E440), Carrageenan (E407)

### E426 Soybean Hemicellulose
⚠️ **ALLERGEN**: Contains soy

### Polysorbates E432-436
⚠️ **SHARED ADI**: All polysorbates share combined ADI of 0-25 mg/kg bw/day  
⚠️ **E433 Controversy**: Some research on gut microbiome effects (ongoing studies)

## Fascinating Facts Per Additive

### E421 Mannitol
- 🌳 Named after biblical "manna from heaven" (sweet tree sap)
- ❄️ Has cooling effect on tongue (like mint)
- 🍬 50-60% as sweet as sugar
- 🏥 Used in kidney function tests
- 💧 Less hygroscopic than other polyols (doesn't absorb water)

### E422 Glycerol ⭐
- 🌍 Most versatile substance on Earth
- 💣 Used to make nitroglycerin (explosives AND heart medicine)
- 📅 Discovered in 1779 during soap-making
- 🧊 Used in antifreeze and de-icing fluids
- 💨 Main ingredient in e-cigarette liquid
- 🔬 Alfred Nobel used it to invent dynamite

### E425 Konjac
- 👹 Called "Devil's tongue" (flower looks like giant purple tongue)
- 🌺 Has one of world's largest flowers, smells like rotting flesh
- 0️⃣ Shirataki noodles = zero calories, 97% water
- 🇯🇵 Traditional Japanese food for 1,000+ years
- 🍜 Cannot be digested by humans - pure soluble fiber

### E426 Soybean Hemicellulose
- ♻️ Made from tofu production waste (okara) - sustainable upcycling
- 🌊 Excellent emulsifier for oil-in-water emulsions
- 🌡️ Stable over wide pH and temperature range

### E427 Cassia Gum
- 🐕 PRIMARY use is pet food (canned dog/cat food)
- ☕ Seeds roasted and used as coffee substitute
- 💰 Cheaper alternative to locust bean gum (E410)

### E428 Gelatin
- 🌡️ Melts at exact body temperature (37°C) - "melts in your mouth"
- 💧 Can hold 10x its weight in water
- 📸 Used in old photography (gelatin silver prints)
- 🐷 60% pork, 30% beef, 10% fish
- 💊 Used for pharmaceutical capsules

### E431 Polyoxyethylene (40) Stearate
- 4️⃣0️⃣ The "40" = 40 units of ethylene oxide
- 💊 More common in cosmetics/pharmaceuticals than food
- 💊 Related to PEG (polyethylene glycol) - laxative ingredient

### E432-436 Polysorbates (Tweens)
- 🔀 "Tween" = in-BETWEEN hydrophilic and lipophilic
- 💉 E432 & E433 used in COVID-19 mRNA vaccines
- 🧠 E433 can help drugs cross blood-brain barrier (research)
- 🍨 E433 prevents ice crystal formation in ice cream
- 3️⃣ E436 is ONLY polysorbate with 3 fatty acid chains (tri-ester)
- 📊 E433 is most widely used polysorbate

## Manufacturing Methods Summary

| E# | Method | Process |

| E426 | Extraction | Water extraction from soybean fiber (okara/tofu waste) |
