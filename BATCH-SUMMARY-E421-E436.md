## Key Data Points

### Polyols (Sugar Alcohols)

#### E421 - Mannitol
- **CAS**: 69-65-8
- **Source**: Natural/synthetic (hydrogenation of fructose)
- **Natural sources**: Mushrooms, seaweed, celery, manna ash tree
- **ADI**: Not specified (safe)
- **Uses**: Sugar-free gum, candies, breath mints
- **Fun fact**: Named after biblical "manna"; has cooling effect on tongue; 50-60% as sweet as sugar

#### E422 - Glycerol (Glycerin)
- **CAS**: 56-81-5
- **Source**: Natural/synthetic (vegetable oils, animal fats, or petrochemical)
- **Natural sources**: Backbone of all fats/oils, all living cells
- **ADI**: Not specified (no upper limit - extremely safe)
- **Uses**: EVERYWHERE - food, cosmetics, antifreeze, explosives
- **Fun fact**: Most versatile substance on Earth; used to make nitroglycerin/dynamite; discovered in 1779 during soap-making

### Natural Thickeners/Gelling Agents

#### E425 - Konjac (Glucomannan)
- **CAS**: 37220-17-0
- **Source**: Plant-derived (konjac root/corm)
- **Natural sources**: Amorphophallus konjac plant (Southeast Asia)
- **ADI**: Not specified
- **Uses**: Shirataki noodles (zero-calorie), konnyaku jelly, vegan candy
- **Regulatory**: Mini-cup jelly BANNED in EU/USA (choking hazard)
- **Fun facts**: 
  - Called "Devil's tongue" - flower looks like giant purple tongue and smells like rotting flesh
  - Traditional Japanese food for 1,000+ years
  - Shirataki noodles = "miracle noodles" (zero calories, 97% water)
  - Cannot be digested - pure soluble fiber

#### E426 - Soybean Hemicellulose
- **CAS**: 9034-32-6
- **Source**: Plant-derived (soybean fiber/okara)
- **ADI**: Not specified
- **Uses**: Beverages, dressings, dairy alternatives
- **Fun fact**: Made from tofu production waste (okara) - sustainable additive
- **Allergen**: Contains soy

#### E427 - Cassia Gum
- **CAS**: 9000-76-4
- **Source**: Plant-derived (cassia seeds)
- **ADI**: 0-1.5 mg/kg bw
- **Uses**: PRIMARILY PET FOOD (canned dog/cat food), some human food
- **Fun fact**: Seeds used as coffee substitute; cheaper alternative to locust bean gum

#### E428 - Gelatin
- **CAS**: 9000-70-8
- **Source**: ANIMAL-DERIVED (pork 60%, beef 30%, fish 10%)
- **ADI**: Not specified (safe)
- **Uses**: Marshmallows, gummy bears, Jello, yogurt, pharmaceutical capsules
- **Religious/Dietary Restrictions**:
  - ❌ **Pork gelatin**: NOT kosher or halal (most common source)
  - ✅ **Beef gelatin**: Kosher/halal if from certified slaughter
  - ✅ **Fish gelatin**: Kosher (pareve), suitable for pescatarians
  - ❌ **ALL gelatin**: NOT vegetarian/vegan
  - **Alternatives**: Agar (E406), Pectin (E440), Carrageenan (E407)
- **Fun facts**:
  - Made from animal collagen (skin, bones, connective tissue)
  - Melts at body temperature (37°C) - "melts in your mouth"
  - Can hold 10x its weight in water
  - Used in old photography (gelatin silver prints)

### Synthetic Emulsifiers

#### E431 - Polyoxyethylene (40) Stearate
- **CAS**: 9004-99-3
- **Source**: Synthetic (stearic acid + PEG-40)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (all PEG stearates)
- **Uses**: Baked goods, desserts, MORE common in cosmetics/pharmaceuticals
- **Note**: "40" = 40 units of ethylene oxide; related to PEG (laxative)

#### E432 - Polysorbate 20 (Tween 20)
- **CAS**: 9005-64-5
- **Source**: Synthetic (sorbitol + lauric acid + ethylene oxide)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (ALL polysorbates combined)
- **Uses**: Ice cream, baked goods, vitamins, COVID-19 vaccines
- **Naming**: "Tween" = in-between hydrophilic and lipophilic; "20" = lauric acid (12 carbons)

#### E433 - Polysorbate 80 (Tween 80) ⭐ MOST COMMON
- **CAS**: 9005-65-6
- **Source**: Synthetic (sorbitol + oleic acid + ethylene oxide)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (ALL polysorbates combined)
- **Uses**: Ice cream (prevents ice crystals), milk, pickles, vaccines
- **Controversy**: Medium - some studies on gut microbiome effects, rare allergic reactions
- **Naming**: "80" = oleic acid (18 carbons, 1 double bond)
- **Fun facts**:
  - Most widely used polysorbate
  - Used in many vaccines and injectable medicines
  - Can help drugs cross blood-brain barrier (research use)

#### E434 - Polysorbate 40 (Tween 40)
- **CAS**: 9005-66-7
- **Source**: Synthetic (sorbitol + palmitic acid + ethylene oxide)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (ALL polysorbates combined)
- **Uses**: Baked goods, frozen desserts, confectionery
- **Naming**: "40" = palmitic acid (16 carbons)
- **Note**: LESS common than Tweens 20, 60, and 80

#### E435 - Polysorbate 60 (Tween 60)
- **CAS**: 9005-67-8
- **Source**: Synthetic (sorbitol + stearic acid + ethylene oxide)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (ALL polysorbates combined)
- **Uses**: Ice cream, cakes, whipped cream, coffee whiteners
- **Naming**: "60" = stearic acid (18 carbons, saturated)
- **Note**: Second most common polysorbate after Tween 80

#### E436 - Polysorbate 65 (Tween 65)
- **CAS**: 9005-71-4
- **Source**: Synthetic (sorbitol + 3× stearic acid + ethylene oxide)
- **ADI**: 0-25 mg/kg bw (ALL polysorbates combined)
- **Uses**: Cake mixes, whipped toppings, bakery products
- **Naming**: "65" = stearic acid with TRI-ester form (only polysorbate with 3 fatty acid chains)
- **Note**: LEAST common polysorbate; more lipophilic (fat-loving) than others
